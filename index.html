The Problem: Manual Quote Processing

Meet Alliance AMS

Alliance AMS is a Perth industrial and automotive supplier run by Director Gavin McGavock. They supply parts, safety equipment, tools, and industrial supplies to mine site workshops and earthmoving companies across Western Australia.

üö® Current Reality

Customers know what they need, but getting quotes takes hours of manual work.

Workshop mechanics send Gavin lists of part numbers from their maintenance logs. Only 2% of business happens through the website - 98% comes via email to Gavin directly. Then the manual processing begins...

Gavin's Daily Quote Process (Hours per Quote)

  1. Customer emails Gavin with list of part numbers they need (often 10-30 parts from different suppliers)
  2. Gavin opens Peach software (his inventory system)
  3. Manually searches EACH part number one at a time in Peach
  4. Checks current pricing by:
    • Opening supplier websites ‚Üí searching for each part ‚Üí checking current price
    • When prices have changed, manually updates them in Peach
    • For Toyota parts: emails Toyota dealer ‚Üí waits for THEM to manually process ‚Üí receives email back with pricing ‚Üí updates Peach
  5. Repeats for EVERY PART from multiple suppliers (automotive, industrial, safety equipment)
  6. Applies customer-specific markup (different customers have different negotiated rates)
  7. Manually creates quote in Peach with all parts and final prices
  8. Generates PDF and emails to customer

For Complex Multi-Supplier Quotes
üí° Even simple quotes take 45+ minutes. Complex quotes with 20-30 parts from multiple suppliers can take 2-3 hours. Despite this, Gavin is STILL faster than his competitors - which shows how broken the industry process is.

Why This Happens

  • Multiple supplier sources - Parts come from different suppliers: automotive, Toyota dealers, industrial, safety equipment. Each has different access methods (websites, APIs, email)
  • Constantly changing prices - Supplier prices update frequently, requiring manual checks for every quote
  • Customer-specific pricing - Different customers have different negotiated markup rates. No automated way to apply the right pricing tier
  • Email-dependent suppliers - Toyota dealer requires email requests and responds with manual quotes, adding delays and extra processing steps
  • Website barely used - Only 2% of business happens online. 98% comes via email because customers can't get quotes themselves
  • No quote automation - Every quote requires human intervention from start to finish
